Privacy Policy

During registration you provide us information about yourself such as name, email address, phone number, and picture. Some of this information is available to users, who are members in the same Plans as you are or to whom a plan, where you are a member, is shared to. We may use your contact information to send you information regarding our services. If you email us, we may keep your message, email address, and contact information to respond to your request.

We collect personally non-identifiable data about you when you use the Site. We may store such information in our offices and servers, or in databases owned and maintained by our contractors and partners. We may use personally non-identifiable data to track the amount of page downloads, site visits, and domain names of our users to track the usage of our Site.

When you visit the Site, it may send one or more "cookies" - a small data file - to your computer to identify your browser in order to improve the user experience. A cookie may convey anonymous information about how you browse the Service to us, but does not collect personal information about you.

2. Sharing information on Plango

Plango is designed for private planning of group activities with friends. Plan is the key concept in the Plango sharing and privacy model. Plan includes information on the activity (title and / or interest category such as dinner or sports), inviteed participants (with Attending, Awaiting, Declined RSVP status), date and time, privacy status, and place data. Only the creator of the plan can change the plan data (excluding the RSVP data) or invite new participants to the plan. Plango provides three mechanisms to share plan data:

1) Creating a plan and inviting other users to the plan.

All invited members can view all plan data, join, leave, and comment a plan. New invited members can see all the previous comments of the plan.

2) Sharing a plan to Plango friends

By default a plan has "Private" status, which means that it can only be viewed, commented, and joined by the invited members. Plans with "Friends" status can also be viewed, commented, and joined by all Plango friends of the creator of the plan.

3) Posting a plan to user's own Facebook wall

Creator of the plan can decide to share the plan on Facebook. When Facebook post is selected, Plango will generate a wall post to the creator's wall including the basic plan details (what, where, when, who). However, the plan itself, including the comments, is not visible to Facebook users.

Plans with "Public" status are shown to all Plango friends of the joined Plango users in those events. Public plans cannot be created with Plango but may be fetched from third-party services.
